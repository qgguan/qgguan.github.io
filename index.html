About Me

I am a dedicated mathematician with over 15 years of experience in both research and teaching. My academic journey has taken me through some of the world's most prestigious institutions, where I've developed a passion for exploring the fundamental structures of our universe through mathematics.

My research focuses on the intersection of Algebraic Topology and Differential Geometry, particularly in applications to theoretical physics. I believe that mathematics is not just a collection of abstract concepts, but a powerful language that helps us understand and describe the world around us.

In my teaching, I strive to make complex mathematical concepts accessible and engaging, fostering critical thinking and problem-solving skills that serve students in all areas of their lives.

Research Interests

My research explores the deep connections between topology and geometry, with applications to theoretical physics. I specialize in developing novel approaches to longstanding problems in these fields.

Algebraic Topology

Investigating higher homotopy groups and their applications to classification problems in topology.

Homotopy Theory Homology Cohomology

Differential Geometry

Research on Riemannian manifolds, curvature flows, and geometric analysis with applications to physics.

Manifolds Curvature Tensor Analysis

Mathematical Physics

Applying topological methods to quantum field theory and string theory frameworks.

Quantum Theory Gauge Theory String Theory

Teaching Philosophy

My approach emphasizes conceptual understanding over rote memorization. I challenge students to think deeply about mathematical structures and their interconnections.

I believe in creating an inclusive classroom environment where students feel comfortable exploring ideas and learning from mistakes.

My goal is to develop students' mathematical intuition and problem-solving abilities that will serve them throughout their careers.
